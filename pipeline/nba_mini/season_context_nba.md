# NBA Season Context — May 2026

This snippet is hand-maintained and prepended to every clue-generation prompt so
clues are framed correctly for the current calendar phase. Refresh weekly (or
sooner when something big shifts).

## Where we are in the calendar

It is mid-May 2026. The NBA is in the **2026 Conference Semifinals**. The First
Round wrapped up roughly two weeks ago; the Conference Finals have not started
yet. Several Semis series are mid-stretch — some at 2-1, some at 2-2 — and
travel days keep collapsing the schedule into back-to-back national TV games.

The **2026 NBA Draft Lottery happened the previous weekend** (the Sunday before
last week). Lottery results — who jumped, who fell, the resulting top-pick
conversation — are still active discourse, especially on r/nba. Treat lottery
storylines as **fresh news**, not historical reference.

**All-Star weekend is well in the rearview** (it was in February). Do not lean
on All-Star material as recent — it is firmly mid-season memory at this point.
Anything from October through January is similarly old news; anything from late
April onward is current playoff material.

## Live storylines worth knowing

- **Victor Wembanyama's first career ejection** happened roughly a week ago and
  is still being chewed over. Clips, hot takes, and "is this a turning point"
  arguments are still appearing in r/nba comments. Wemby ejection references
  can land without heavy setup.
- **Lottery aftermath:** the team that jumped into the top pick (and the team
  that fell out) are the current "front-office discourse" subjects. Treat draft
  prospects as named-but-not-yet-NBA: clues can mention them, but the audience
  may not have strong opinions on names beyond the consensus top three.
- **Conference Semis stars:** any 30+ point game, late-game block, or
  questionable foul call from this round is fair game and will resonate with
  yesterday-was-game-day readers.

## Tone guidance for clues

- **Recent events get specifics.** A dunk, ejection, or buzzer-beater from
  yesterday or this week can be referenced by detail — opponent, building,
  score — and the audience will recognize it. Wry and discoursey voices should
  lean on this freshness.
- **Historical events need scaffolding.** A reference to a 2013 Finals moment,
  a 2016 trade, or a 1990s player needs a context clue ("'90s," "Showtime
  era," "pre-LeBron Cleveland"). Do not assume the audience snaps to old
  context the way they do to last night's box score.
- **Playoff framing is implicit.** A 40-point game in May reads as a *playoff*
  40-point game; clues do not need to spell that out. If a referenced game is
  regular-season, the clue should say so.
- **Lottery vs. draft vs. free agency:** these are three different things. We
  are in lottery aftermath. The actual draft is still in June. Free agency is
  even further out. Do not conflate them.
