# WNBA Season Context — May 2026

This snippet is hand-maintained and prepended to every clue-generation prompt so
clues are framed correctly for the current calendar phase. Refresh weekly (or
sooner when something big shifts).

## Where we are in the calendar

It is mid-May 2026. The **2026 WNBA regular season just opened**. Tip-off was
last week. Teams are 1–3 games in. Rosters have settled after training camp
cuts; rotations are still being figured out, and the early-season "is this team
real?" narratives are forming.

This is the **second post-Caitlin-Clark-rookie-year** season. The 2025 season
already ran with her as a sophomore; she's now an established star, no longer
"the rookie." Treat her accordingly — current All-Star, not novelty.

The **Golden State Valkyries** are the league's newest team (their inaugural
season was 2025). They've now played a full season; this is year two. Their
identity is forming — playoff hopeful or rebuild project depending on the take.

## Live storylines worth knowing

- **Aces dynasty era:** Las Vegas remains the team to beat. A'ja Wilson's
  three-MVP run looms over every preview. Any "can anyone unseat them" framing
  is current discourse.
- **Liberty as the chasers:** Stewie + Sabrina + Jonquel form the most-cited
  challenger to the Aces. The 2024 Finals rematch motif is well-worn but real.
- **Caitlin Clark / Indiana Fever:** every televised Fever game is a referendum
  on the league's growth. Court attendance is up across the league because of
  her schedule effect.
- **Expansion arena buzz:** the Valkyries' Chase Center games and the league's
  general "we are bigger than ever" narrative is the macro storyline.
- **Coaching changes from the off-season** are still settling — any new HC
  references should be framed as "first season at the helm" rather than
  established.

## Tone guidance for clues

- **Recent events get specifics.** A clutch shot, dunk, technical foul, or
  ejection from yesterday or this week can be referenced by detail — opponent,
  building, score — and the audience will recognize it. Wry and discoursey
  voices should lean on this freshness.
- **Historical events need scaffolding.** A reference to a 2008 Finals moment,
  a Diana Taurasi prime-era game, or a 1990s player needs a context clue
  ("Houston Comets era," "pre-Aces dynasty"). Do not assume the audience snaps
  to old context the way they do to last night's box score.
- **Regular-season framing is the default in May.** A 30-point game in May is a
  regular-season 30-point game; clues do not need to spell that out unless
  it's a marquee matchup (Aces-Liberty, Fever home opener).
- **Cross-league references are fair but rare.** A clue can lean on the WNBA
  audience knowing NBA context (a player's brother is in the NBA, a coach
  came from the men's side), but don't make it a crutch. Most clues should
  live entirely inside the WNBA universe.
- **First-name basis is more common than NBA.** Caitlin, A'ja, Sabrina, Sue,
  Stewie — first-name references read naturally in WNBA discourse in a way
  that "Bron" only sometimes does in NBA discourse. Lean into it.
